# Exporting Transcripts

## Quick Answer
Click the Export button in the transcript view and choose a format: TXT for plain text, SRT/VTT for subtitles, or DOCX/PDF for documents.

## Step-by-Step

1. Open a recording's transcript view
2. Click the **Export** button in the toolbar
3. Select your desired format
4. Choose export options (if available)
5. Save the file to your computer

## Export Formats

### TXT (Plain Text)
- Simple text file
- Optional: include timestamps
- Best for: quick sharing, copying text

### SRT (SubRip Subtitle)
- Standard subtitle format
- Includes timing for each segment
- Best for: adding subtitles to video

### VTT (WebVTT)
- Web-compatible subtitle format
- Includes timing and optional styling
- Best for: web video players

### DOCX (Word Document)
- Formatted document with speakers and timestamps
- Best for: editing in Microsoft Word, sharing professionally

### PDF
- Formatted, read-only document
- Best for: archiving, printing, formal distribution

## UI Location
- **Page**: Recording detail / Transcript view
- **Button**: Export (in toolbar)

## Tips
- SRT and VTT are interchangeable for most video editors
- DOCX preserves speaker labels and formatting
- Export multiple recordings by selecting them in the Recordings list
- Highlights and comments are not included in exports
